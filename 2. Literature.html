Hamlet

William Shakespeare — Drama • Circa 1600

A famous tragedy set in Denmark about Prince Hamlet seeking revenge for his father's murder by his uncle Claudius, exploring themes of betrayal, madness, and mortality, culminating in widespread death at Elsinore Castle, including Hamlet's own, after a poisoned due.

DramaClassic
Read
More details
Genre: A tragedy, a serious play with a sad ending, a hallmark of Shakespearean drama.
Setting: Primarily Elsinore Castle in Denmark, home to the Danish royal family.
Plot: The ghost of Hamlet's father reveals he was murdered by his brother Claudius (now king and married to Hamlet's mother), prompting Hamlet to feign madness and plot revenge, leading to a spiral of death.
Major Themes: Revenge, deception, appearance vs. reality, madness, mortality, and political intrigue.
Key Characters: Prince Hamlet, King Claudius, Queen Gertrude, Polonius, Ophelia, Laertes, and Horatio.
Style: Written in blank verse (unrhymed iambic pentameter) with complex language and philosophical depth.
Significance: Considered one of the greatest and most influential works in English literature, examining the complexities of human action and inaction.
